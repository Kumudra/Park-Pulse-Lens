Our Group's Story

How we took small steps to make our park cleaner and raise awareness

Real Experience

From Concern to Action

The Problem We Saw

We noticed our local park had increasing amounts of litter - especially plastic bottles, food wrappers, and cigarette butts. It was affecting both the park's beauty and wildlife.

Most common: Plastic bottles & snack wrappers
Trash accumulated over weekends
Affecting local plants and animals
What We Actually Did
1
Weekend Cleanup Sessions

Every Saturday for a month, we spent 2 hours picking up trash in our local park. We separated recyclables from general waste.

5 bags collected 40% recyclable
2
Photo Documentation

We took "before and after" photos to show the difference our cleanups made. The visual evidence was powerful!

10+ photos Visual proof of impact
3
Awareness Posters

We created simple posters about proper waste disposal and placed them near park trash cans.

3 posters made Strategic locations
4
Social Media Sharing

Shared our cleanup photos and tips on Instagram and Facebook to encourage others to do the same.

3+ shares Local community engaged
What We Learned
Small Actions Matter

A few hours each week made visible improvements. We didn't need to be experts to make a difference.

People Notice

Park visitors thanked us and some even joined! Our actions inspired others to be more mindful.

Sustainable Habits

We became more aware of our own waste habits and started carrying reusable bags and bottles.

Community Connection

Working together strengthened our group and connected us with park regulars.

Challenges We Faced
Weather dependent: Rainy days limited our cleanup sessions
Some litter was hazardous: Had to be extra careful with broken glass and sharp objects
Consistency: Keeping the momentum going required commitment
Advice for Other Students

"Start small. Pick one area you care about, gather a few friends, and commit to regular sessions. Document your progress - photos are motivating!"

Wear gloves and use trash grabbers for safety
Separate recyclables to maximize impact
Take before/after photos to track progress
Share your story - it inspires others!
