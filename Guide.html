How to Sort

Click on each card to learn proper sorting techniques

Plastics

DO

  • Clean bottles & containers
  • Rigid plastics with symbols
  • Remove caps and rinse

DON'T

  • Plastic bags in recycling
  • Food contaminated items
  • Styrofoam packaging
Tip: Rinse and flatten to save space

Paper

DO

  • Newspapers & magazines
  • Cardboard boxes (flatten)
  • Office paper & envelopes

DON'T

  • Greasy pizza boxes
  • Wax paper or receipts
  • Tissue paper or napkins
Tip: Remove staples and tape

Glass

DO

  • Bottles & jars (all colors)
  • Clean glass containers
  • Remove lids separately

DON'T

  • Windows or mirrors
  • Ceramics or dishware
  • Light bulbs
Tip: Don't break glass

Metals

DO

  • Aluminum cans (crush)
  • Tin/steel food cans
  • Clean aluminum foil

DON'T

  • Aerosol cans (full)
  • Scrap metal pieces
  • Paint cans with residue
Tip: Crush cans to save space

Organics

DO

  • Fruit/vegetable scraps
  • Coffee grounds & filters
  • Eggshells & nutshells

DON'T

  • Meat, fish, or dairy
  • Cooking oils or grease
  • Pet waste or litter
Tip: Use compostable bags

Electronics

DO

  • Phones & tablets
  • Computers & cables
  • Batteries (separate)

DON'T

  • In regular trash
  • In curbside recycling
  • Break or dismantle
Tip: Find e-waste drop-off

Textiles

DO

  • Clean clothing (donate)
  • Shoes (paired together)
  • Linens & towels

DON'T

  • Wet or moldy items
  • Carpeting or rugs
  • Pillows or cushions
Tip: Donate wearable items

Hazardous

DO

  • Special drop-off only
  • Paint & chemicals
  • Batteries & bulbs

DON'T

  • In regular trash
  • Down drains or toilets
  • In recycling bins
Tip: Check community waste days

Myth Busting

Separating fact from fiction in eco-awareness

Myth

"All plastic can be recycled"

Fact

Only plastics with recycling symbols 1, 2, and 5 are widely recyclable. Plastic bags and Styrofoam need special handling.

Myth

"Recycling uses more energy than it saves"

Fact

Recycling aluminum saves 95% energy vs new production. Paper recycling saves 40% energy and reduces water usage by 58%.

Myth

"Biodegradable products break down in landfills"

Fact

Landfills lack oxygen for proper decomposition. Most biodegradable items need commercial composting facilities.

Quick Quiz

Is it better to recycle plastic with food residue or throw it away?

Answer: Rinse lightly and recycle! Food contamination ruins entire batches of recycling.

Park Care Challenges

Simple responsibilities you should do in any park

Poop Patrol

01. Bring waste bags when walking your dog.
02. Pick up and properly dispose of pet waste.
03. Encourage others to do the same.


Note: Clean parks = happy paws and people! Perfect for responsible pet owners.
Trash Treasure Hunt

01. Grab gloves and a small trash bag.
02. Collect litter as you stroll through the park.
03. Sort recyclables if possible.


Note: Every piece picked up helps wildlife and keeps nature beautiful! Great for eco-heroes
